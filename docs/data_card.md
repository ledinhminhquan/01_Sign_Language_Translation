# P01 — Sign Language Translation · Data Card

**Author:** Le Dinh Minh Quan (23127460) · NLP in Industry, Final Assignment.
**Package:** `signlang` · **Folder:** `01_Sign_Language_Translation/`.

This data card documents every data source touched by P01, the **synthetic pose-sequence generator** that is the
project's *primary, non-negotiable* offline data, and the licensing landscape that *defines* the field. The headline
fact, verified on the Hugging Face Hub (authenticated as `ledinhminhquan`), is structural rather than incidental:

> **There is no permissively-licensed, directly-loadable continuous Sign Language Translation corpus on the Hub.**
> Every continuous-signing benchmark is **non-commercial, gated, or unspecified**, and the canonical academic corpora
> (PHOENIX-2014T, CSL-Daily, YouTube-ASL) are **not redistributable Hub repos** at all. This is not a gap in our
> research; it is the field's defining constraint, and it is *why* the trainable core is driven by a synthetic
> generator with embedded gold rather than by any single real corpus.

Because this task maps **sign-language video / pose-keypoint sequences → spoken-language text** (via an intermediate
**gloss** sequence, the Sign2Gloss2Text cascade), the data here is fundamentally different from the prior 18 text/OCR
projects: it is **biometric, temporal, multimodal** (video frames or 3-D landmark trajectories), and tied to a specific
human community (the Deaf community). The licensing and bias notes below are correspondingly more serious.

## 2. The synthetic pose-sequence generator (PRIMARY data)

**Module:** `data/synth_pose.py` · **Vocabulary:** `data/lexicon.py` · **Keypoint layout:** `pose/layout.py`.

This is the spine of the project. It mirrors the embedded-gold synthetic-generator pattern proven in P15/P17/P19/P20,
but produces **motion trajectories in keypoint space** rather than text or images. It exists because every real
continuous-SLT corpus is restrictively licensed, so a reproducible, license-clean, deterministic generator that embeds
its own gold labels is both the defensible offline data and an honest design choice.

### 2.1 Modality and schema

- **Modality:** sequences of per-frame **pose-keypoint vectors** — the same representation MediaPipe Holistic and
  `PSewmuthu/How2Sign_Holistic` emit, so the offline schema is wire-compatible with the real Colab front-end.
- **Keypoint layout (`pose/layout.py`):** **2 × 21 hand landmarks + 25 body landmarks**, each with **3 coordinates**
  (x, y, z) → a fixed-width per-frame vector. This deliberately mirrors MediaPipe Holistic's hand+body landmark counts
  so that swapping `SeedPoseEngine` (offline) for MediaPipe (Colab) needs no schema change downstream.
- **A sequence** = a concatenation of **2–6 signs**, each sign being a short keypoint sub-trajectory, signs separated by
  **near-still rest frames**.
- **Embedded gold:** each generated sequence carries an embedded spec `{glosses, text, boundaries}` — the gold gloss
  list, the gold spoken-language text, and the gold sign-boundary frame indices. `SeedPoseEngine` and `SeedRecognizer`
  read this back, so segmentation, recognition, translation, evaluation, the agent, and the tests all run with **no
  MediaPipe, no torch, no video, and no network**.

### 2.2 Determinism (reproducibility contract)

- For each of the **40 glosses** in `data/lexicon.py`, a **deterministic motion direction** in keypoint space is fixed,
  seeded by the gloss index. The same gloss always moves along the same direction.
- A single sign is a **smooth up-down (triangle) stroke** along that direction, so the per-sign **mean displacement**
  uniquely recovers the gloss — this is what the offline numpy nearest-centroid classifier genuinely classifies (it is
  *not* reading the gold for recognition; it classifies the displacement signal).
- Signs are separated by **low-velocity rest frames**, so a motion-based segmenter can split a continuous sequence into
  sign units by detecting velocity troughs (this is what the D2 segmenter and the boundary-F1 metric exercise).
- Given a fixed seed and config, the generator is **byte-for-byte reproducible**: same vocabulary, same trajectories,
  same train/dev/test split. This is the property that makes the metric story auditable.

### 2.3 Vocabulary and the gloss→text map

- **40 ASL-style glosses** with a curated **gloss→text lexicon**. The spoken text is **deliberately not** the gloss
  tokens: e.g. `THANK-YOU` → "thank you", `ME` → "i". This makes the **translation stage non-trivial vs identity** — the
  model must learn casing, multi-word expansion, function-word insertion, and reordering, not just copy tokens. The
  measured "identity-translate" baseline (gloss tokens emitted verbatim as text) quantifies exactly how much the lexicon
  and reordering add (~15 BLEU in offline verification).
- Verified offline behaviour on the clean synthetic split: **gloss accuracy 1.0, BLEU 99+, segmentation-F1 1.0** against
  a most-frequent-gloss floor of **0.02** and an identity-translate BLEU of **~84**; pose-noise injection degrades
  recognition (the robustness sweep), and **pure-noise input abstains**. All five agent decision points fire.

### 2.4 Honest limitations vs real signing

The generator is a **measurable proxy, not a model of human signing**. It deliberately does *not* claim phonological or
linguistic realism. Concretely it omits:

- **Non-manual features** — facial expression, mouthing, eye gaze, head tilt, and torso shifts that carry grammatical
  meaning (negation, questions, topic marking) in every natural sign language. Our layout includes body landmarks but
  **no face landmarks and no learned non-manual semantics**.
- **Coarticulation and transitions** — real signs blend into one another (movement epenthesis); rest-frame separation is
  a simplification that makes segmentation cleaner than reality.
- **Signer variation** — handedness, signing speed, dialect, body proportions, and individual style. Every synthetic
  signer is identical, so the offline numbers **overstate** signer-independent performance.
- **Spatial grammar and classifiers** — use of signing space for referents, agreement verbs, and classifier
  constructions, which have no fixed lexical motion direction.
- **Real-world noise** — occlusion, motion blur, lighting, camera angle, dropped landmarks, and tracking jitter. These
  are approximated only by the synthetic pose-noise sweep, not reproduced.

**Consequence:** synthetic results validate the *plumbing* (segment → recognize → translate → verify → abstain) and the
relative ordering of baselines vs the trained core. They are **not** evidence of real-language SLT quality. The
`Sigurdur/icelandic-sign-language` smoke test and the Colab MediaPipe path exist precisely to keep this honest.

## 3. Real datasets — permissive (✅ used)

### 3.1 `Sigurdur/icelandic-sign-language`

- **Role:** the **only cleanly permissive real corpus** on the Hub for this task → real-data **smoke test** for the
  front-end and the end-to-end path (does the pipeline ingest real timed transcripts and produce sane output?).
- **Modality / schema:** an Icelandic slice of **YouTube-SL-25**; each row has a **`video_id`** and a **timed
  `transcript`** (start/end + text). Video is referenced by id, not redistributed in the repo.
- **Size:** **214 rows** — tiny; sufficient for a smoke test, not for training.
- **License:** **Apache-2.0** ✅ (commercial-safe).
- **Provenance:** derived from the YouTube-SL-25 effort; transcripts are timed captions over public YouTube sign-language
  video.
- **Known biases / coverage:** **single sign language (Icelandic)**, small signer set, caption-derived text (not gloss).
  Models or sanity checks built on it do **not** transfer to other sign languages. Continuous, not isolated.

### 3.2 `om192006/sign_language_keypoints`

- **Role:** pose-schema template and an isolated-gesture sanity reference (confirms our `pose/layout.py` keypoint
  ordering matches a real extracted-keypoint dataset).
- **Modality / schema:** **pre-extracted MediaPipe keypoints** (no raw video) for a small gesture set.
- **Size:** **29 gestures** — isolated, tiny.
- **License:** **MIT** ✅.
- **Known biases / coverage:** **isolated signs only** (no continuous signing, no sentence structure), narrow vocabulary,
  unspecified signer diversity. Useful for schema, not for translation training.

## 4. Real datasets — FLAGGED (referenced for schema / precedent, NOT used to train)

These are documented for provenance and to justify the architecture. **None is redistributed in this repo, and none is
used to train the measured component.** Each flag is load-bearing for the ethics and licensing posture.

### 4.1 `PSewmuthu/How2Sign_Holistic` — ⚠️ FLAG: MIT tag, **NC upstream**

- **Role:** the landmark-layout reference our `pose/layout.py` mirrors (MediaPipe Holistic landmark sequences as `.npy`).
- **Modality:** MediaPipe Holistic landmark sequences (hand + body + face), pre-extracted.
- **License conflict:** the **repo carries an MIT tag, but it is derived from How2Sign, whose upstream license is
  CC-BY-NC**. A derived-pose repo **cannot grant more permissive terms than its source** — treat the *effective* license
  as **non-commercial**. Use only as a schema reference; do not train on it for any commercial deployment.

### 4.2 `aipieces/How2Sign` — ⚠️ FLAG: **CC-BY-NC upstream, unspecified repo license**

- **Role:** reference for what a continuous ASL→English corpus looks like (video + keypoints).
- **Modality:** continuous ASL video with English translations and keypoints.
- **License:** repo license **unspecified**; **upstream How2Sign is CC-BY-NC** → **non-commercial** effectively. An
  unspecified Hub license is *not* a grant of rights — treat as all-rights-reserved at minimum, NC given the source.

### 4.3 `Exploration-Lab/iSign` — ⚠️ FLAG: **CC-BY-NC-SA + GATED**

- **Role:** the dataset that **defines SignPose2Text** — *this exact task* (Indian Sign Language pose → text). Cited as
  the task-definition precedent; it is why our cascade is framed as pose→gloss→text.
- **Modality / size:** Indian Sign Language; pose + text; **118K+ pairs**.
- **License:** **CC-BY-NC-SA** (non-commercial **and** ShareAlike) **and GATED** (access requires accepting terms /
  approval). Triple constraint: non-commercial, viral ShareAlike, and gated access. Not usable as redistributable
  training data here; cited for task framing only.
- **Known biases / coverage:** single sign language (ISL); signer/region coverage as documented upstream. Continuous.

### 4.4 `Kibalama/poseformer-sign-language` — ⚠️ FLAG: WLASL-derived, non-permissive source

- **Role:** isolated-sign landmark reference (PoseFormer-style WLASL → MediaPipe landmarks).
- **License:** **unset**; derived from **WLASL ("other")** → cannot be treated as permissive. Isolated signs only.

### 4.5 `Voxel51/WLASL` — ⚠️ FLAG: research terms, recognition-only

- **Role:** the canonical **isolated**-sign recognition reference.
- **Modality / size:** **11,980 isolated-sign videos**.
- **License:** **`other`** — research/academic terms, **not** a permissive open license. Isolated-sign **recognition**
  only (no continuous translation, no spoken-text targets). Documented for completeness; not used.

## 5. NOT on the Hub — academic corpora (cited, **never** used, **do not invent ids**)

The benchmarks the SLT literature is built on are **not redistributable Hugging Face repos**. They live on university
servers under academic licenses requiring individual agreements. They are named here only so the design's
license-driven reasoning is auditable — **no Hub id should ever be fabricated for them.**

- **RWTH-PHOENIX-Weather-2014T** — German Sign Language weather forecasts; the canonical continuous-SLT benchmark
  (Camgöz et al.). Academic license, RWTH Aachen servers. Not on the Hub.
- **CSL-Daily** — Chinese Sign Language daily-life corpus. Academic license. Not on the Hub.
- **YouTube-ASL / YouTube-SL-25** — large web-scraped ASL / multilingual sets; the *Icelandic slice* surfaces as the
  permissive `Sigurdur/icelandic-sign-language`, but the **full corpora are not clean redistributable Hub repos**.

## 6. Intended use

- **Primary intended use:** an **educational / research demonstration** of a license-honest Sign2Gloss2Text pipeline —
  pose front-end → motion segmentation → per-segment gloss recognition → gloss→text translation → verification →
  **abstention** — measured end-to-end on the synthetic spine, smoke-tested on one permissive real corpus.
- **Appropriate users:** the project author and graders; engineers studying SLT system design, license-constrained ML,
  or abstention-based agentic pipelines.
- **Appropriate deployment:** offline CPU path (`SeedPoseEngine` + numpy centroid + lexicon) for reproducible evaluation;
  the Colab path (MediaPipe Holistic + transformer recognizer + `t5-small`) for real-pose experimentation, with
  **per-sign confidence shown and low-confidence output abstained**, never presented as authoritative.

## 7. Out-of-scope use

- **Any production or safety-critical interpretation** — medical, legal, emergency, or financial settings. SLT here is an
  **assistive aid, not a replacement for a qualified human interpreter**. A low-confidence translation must never be
  surfaced as authoritative; the agent's abstention and per-sign confidence exist to enforce this.
- **Claiming real-language competence from synthetic results.** The synthetic numbers validate the pipeline's mechanics,
  not human-signing accuracy (see §2.4).
- **Cross-language / cross-signer generalization.** Representation bias is acute: a model trained on one sign language or
  signer set **fails on others**. Nothing here is signer-independent or multilingual.
- **Commercial use of any flagged dataset** (How2Sign / How2Sign_Holistic, iSign, WLASL, poseformer-sign-language) — all
  effectively non-commercial, gated, or research-terms (§4). Commercial paths must use the permissive backbones
  (`t5-small`, `m2m100`, MediaPipe) plus the synthetic generator and the Apache/MIT permissive corpora only.
- **Biometric profiling or identification.** Sign-language video is **biometric and identifying** (face + hands, Deaf
  community data). No retention by default; on-device/edge processing; the optional LLM brain is OFF by default.

## 8. Provenance, licensing, and ethics summary

- **What we redistribute:** only the **synthetic generator** and its config (MIT, with the repo). No real dataset is
  vendored.
- **What we depend on permissively:** `Sigurdur/icelandic-sign-language` (Apache-2.0) and `om192006/sign_language_keypoints`
  (MIT) for smoke tests and schema; `t5-small` / `byt5-small` / `m2m100_418M` and MediaPipe for the model stack (see the
  Model Selection / Model Card docs).
- **What we flag and avoid for training/redistribution:** every continuous corpus (How2Sign and its Holistic derivative,
  iSign, WLASL, poseformer-sign-language) — **non-commercial, gated, or unspecified**. Effective license follows the
  most restrictive link in the chain, and an unspecified Hub license is treated as all-rights-reserved.
- **What does not exist on the Hub:** PHOENIX-2014T, CSL-Daily, YouTube-ASL — cited, not fetched, never given a fake id.
- **Bias posture:** all real sign-language data here is **single-language, small-signer-set, and mostly isolated or
  caption-derived**. Coverage is narrow on language, region, signer demographics, and signing register. This is stated
  plainly so no result is over-claimed.

> **Evaluation honesty caveat (carried through every doc):** automatic SLT metrics — BLEU/chrF/ROUGE/BLEURT — are
> **unreliable**: length-sensitive and blind to hallucination and semantic equivalence (Yazdani et al., 2025,
> hf.co/papers/2510.25434). We report the standard set **and flag this limitation** wherever a number appears.
